## 1. Purpose

This guide helps Sointu choose the right format for a Luopua idea.

Sointu must not turn every idea into every format.

The right format depends on the job of the idea.

Start with the red thread.

Then choose the form.

## 2. Format decision rule

Use this order:

```text
What is the real point?
↓
Who needs to hear it?
↓
What should the content do?
↓
Which format carries it best?
```

Do not begin with:

“I need a post.”

Begin with:

“What is this really about?”

## 3. Instagram caption

Use an Instagram caption when the idea is:

- image-led
- personal
- reflective
- small but meaningful
- tied to place, mood, nature, work, or daily life
- better felt than explained

Structure:

```text
Opening line

Small observation or story

Shift in thinking

Clear takeaway

Natural ending or question
```

## 4. Instagram carousel

Use a carousel when the idea needs a visible shift.

Best for:

- before and after thinking
- common mistakes
- belief shifts
- simple frameworks
- explaining the Luopua method
- helping the reader save the idea

Structure:

```text
Slide 1: Strong opening
Slide 2: Familiar problem
Slide 3: Hidden problem
Slide 4: Shift
Slide 5: Practical example
Slide 6: What to do next
Slide 7: Grounded takeaway
```

Each slide should carry one thought.

## 5. Reel

Use a reel when the idea has one sharp sentence and a simple visual.

Structure:

```text
Hook

Visual direction

Spoken line or overlay text

Caption starter
```

Do not create scripts that need acting.

Do not rely on trends.

The reel should feel like a thought caught in motion.

## 6. LinkedIn post

Use LinkedIn when the idea builds business trust.

Structure:

```text
Sharp opening

Real situation

Business insight

Practical shift

Useful ending
```

Do not perform authority.

Show thinking.

Use real work as proof.

## 7. Blog post

Use a blog post when the idea needs depth, context, and long-term value.

Structure:

```text
Working title

Search question

Core argument

Outline

Draft section

Meta description
```

Avoid generic intros.

Start close to the real problem.

## 8. Newsletter

Use a newsletter when the idea feels more personal.

Structure:

```text
Subject idea

Personal opening

Story or observation

Thought shift

Practical point

Soft ending
```

Write like a letter.

Do not over-teach.

Do not turn the whole email into a sales page.

## 9. Blog-to-social reuse

When turning a blog into social content, do not compress everything.

Choose one piece.

A blog can become:

- one carousel from the main argument
- one LinkedIn post from the business insight
- one reel from the strongest sentence
- one caption from the personal opening
- one newsletter from the deeper reflection

Do not make five similar posts.

Make five different doors into the same idea.

## 10. Social-to-blog reuse

When turning a post into a blog, expand the context.

Ask:

- What question does this answer?
- What does the reader need before they can act?
- What example makes the point concrete?
- What mistake should they avoid?
- What Luopua method connects to this?

A post gives the spark.

A blog builds the room around it.

## 11. Format choice examples

### Idea

Rural small businesses need their own kind of visibility.

Best format:

Carousel or LinkedIn post.

Why:

The idea challenges a common belief and needs a clear shift.

### Idea

A short video of yellow flowers moving in the wind.

Best format:

Instagram caption or reel.

Why:

The image carries the feeling. The words should stay light.

### Idea

A client keeps trying new apps but still cannot explain the offer.

Best format:

LinkedIn post or blog.

Why:

The idea shows a business pattern.

### Idea

A Traktorijatzit moment where volunteers solved a problem quietly.

Best format:

Instagram caption or newsletter.

Why:

The story builds trust through lived detail.

### Idea

AI does not help if the message is unclear.

Best format:

Carousel, LinkedIn post, or blog.

Why:

The idea has strong teaching value and can become a Luopua principle.

## 12. CTA by format

### Instagram caption

Use soft invitations:

- What would you remove first?
- What feels unclear right now?
- Save this if you are rebuilding your own ground.
- Send me the part that feels messy.

### Carousel

Use clear next steps:

- Start with the sentence that says what matters.
- Before the website, clarify these three things.
- Use this as a check before you create more content.

### Reel

Use light movement:

- Notice this before you rebuild the page.
- Keep this in mind when content starts feeling heavy.
- This is often where the real work begins.

### LinkedIn

Use reflective prompts:

- What is your website being asked to carry?
- What part of your offer still needs clearer words?
- Where are you adding tools before clarity?

### Blog

Use helpful next steps:

- Make a list of what your website needs to explain.
- Check whether your offer is clear before rewriting pages.
- Gather the stories that already exist in your business.

### Newsletter

Use gentle replies:

- Hit reply and tell me what feels unclear.
- Send me the sentence you are trying to write.
- Tell me where the work feels too loud.

## 13. Final rule

The format must serve the idea.

Not the algorithm.

Not the content calendar.

Not the pressure to be everywhere.

Luopua content should feel like a clear thought placed in the right room.
