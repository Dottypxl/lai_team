# Silta – Collaboration Message Library

## 1. Purpose

This file gives Silta reusable collaboration message patterns.

Use these for:

- project invitations
- partner follow-ups
- village project messages
- cultural project messages
- local business collaborations
- thank-you notes
- warm relationship messages
- planning meeting invitations

## 2. Collaboration rule

A collaboration message should make three things clear:

```text
Why you
Why this
Why now
```

Then make the next step easy.

## 3. Collaboration invitation pattern

### Template

Subject:

Possible collaboration around [project]

Message:

Hi [Name],

I am working on [project] and thought of you because [specific reason].

The project is about [short description].

I think there may be a natural connection because [shared context].

The possible role could be [simple role].

The first step would be [clear next step].

Would you be open to [ask]?

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 4. Collaboration invitation example: rural tourism project

Subject:

Possible collaboration around a rural tourism project

Hi [Name],

I am working on an idea for a rural tourism project that would bring together villages, small businesses, culture, and place-based stories in Central Finland.

I thought of you because your work already connects visitors with local experiences.

I think there may be a natural connection because the project needs partners who understand both the visitor side and the village side.

The possible role could be joining the first planning discussion and helping define what would make this useful for businesses.

Would you be open to a short call?

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 5. Village association invitation

The goal is to understand what would be useful, realistic, and worth building together.

Would someone from your association be able to join the first discussion?

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 15. Collaboration message openings

Use:

- I am writing because there may be a natural connection between your work and this project.
- I thought of you because [specific reason].
- I am putting together the first partners for [project].
- I would like to invite you into an early conversation about [project].
- This is still early, but I think your perspective would be useful.

Avoid:

- I hope this finds you well.
- I wanted to reach out.
- We are excited to offer you.
- I am thrilled to announce.
- As a valued stakeholder.

## 16. Collaboration message endings

Use:

- Would you be open to a short call?
- Would this be relevant for you?
- Can I send a one-page summary?
- Would someone from your team like to join the first discussion?
- If this is not the right fit, no problem.
- I would be happy to keep the connection open.

## 17. Collaboration quality check

Before sending, check:

- Is the reason for contact specific?
- Is the project explained simply?
- Is the possible role clear?
- Is the first step easy?
- Is the message too long?
- Does the tone match the relationship?
- Does it sound like Kirsi?
- Is there any fake flattery?
- Can one paragraph be removed?

## 18. Final rule

Collaboration starts with clear context.

Not with a vague request to work together.
