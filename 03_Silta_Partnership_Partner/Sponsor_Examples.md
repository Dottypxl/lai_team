# Silta – Sponsor Examples

## 1. Purpose

This file gives Silta sponsor message examples.

Sponsor messages should be clear, respectful, and grounded.

The goal is to show why support makes sense.

Not to beg.

Not to promise what has not been agreed.

## 2. Sponsor message rules

Use:

- short event or project context
- real reason for contact
- clear support ask
- concrete sponsor value
- clear next step
- honest limits

Avoid:

- inflated reach claims
- vague visibility promises
- guilt
- over-praising the sponsor
- long history before the ask
- formal sponsor jargon
- fake deadline pressure

## 3. Sponsor message structure

```text
Subject

Short context

Why this sponsor fits

The ask

What support helps make possible

What the sponsor receives

Next step
```

## 4. Example: local beef producer for Traktorijatzit

### Subject options

- Sponsor idea for Traktorijatzit 2026
- Local food and Traktorijatzit
- A possible Traktorijatzit partnership
- Supporting village culture through Traktorijatzit

### Message

Hi [Name],

I am writing about Traktorijatzit 2026.

We are looking for a few partners whose work fits the spirit of the event: local place, summer, live music, and people gathering in Suovanlahti.

I thought of your beef products because they are local, recognizable, and connected to the same kind of trust that carries a village event.

Would you be open to discussing a small sponsor partnership?

One option could be product support or a sponsor package where your business is visible at the event and in selected event communication.

The support would help us keep building a village festival that brings people to Suovanlahti and keeps local culture visible.

If this feels relevant, I can send the sponsor options and we can see what level would make sense.

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 11. Sponsor package wording

Use this language when describing options.

### Small support

For businesses that want to support the event in a simple, visible way.

Includes agreed event or online visibility.

### Product support

For businesses that want to support the event through products, materials, food, services, or practical help.

Visibility is agreed based on the support.

### Main partner

For businesses that want stronger visibility and a closer connection to the festival.

Includes broader agreed visibility before and during the event.

## 12. Sponsor value phrases

Use:

- visible support for local culture
- connection to a village event with a loyal audience
- visibility among people who value live music, summer, and place
- a natural way to support community-led work
- a place in the story of a long-running rural event
- support for volunteer-built culture
- local visibility that feels grounded

Avoid:

- massive exposure
- high-value audience access
- brand lift
- performance metrics unless known
- guaranteed reach
- conversion
- audience capture

## 13. Sponsor checklist

Before sending, check:

- Is the event or project clear?
- Is the support ask clear?
- Is the sponsor fit real?
- Is the return honest?
- Are any facts verified?
- Is there a clear next step?
- Is the tone respectful?
- Is the message short enough?
- Does it avoid begging?
- Does it avoid inflated promises?

## 14. Final rule

A sponsor message should make the fit visible and the ask clear.

That is enough.
