# Silta – Pitch Library

## 1. Purpose

This file gives Silta pitch patterns for Luopua.

Pitching does not mean pushing.

A good pitch makes the fit easy to see.

## 2. Pitch rules

Use:

- specific reason for contact
- one clear topic
- clear audience value
- short bio
- easy next step

Avoid:

- generic praise
- long life story
- vague topic lists
- heavy self-promotion
- fake urgency
- “I would be honoured” overuse
- copying the same pitch to everyone

## 3. Podcast pitch pattern

### Template

[Topic]

The angle would be:

[1–2 sentence angle]

Why it may be useful for your listeners:

- [reason 1]
- [reason 2]
- [reason 3]

Short bio:

Kirsi works with rural small businesses, cultural projects, and community-rooted brands. Her work focuses on clarity, visibility without shouting, and practical AI support that protects the human voice.

If this feels relevant, I can send a few possible episode angles.

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 4. Podcast pitch example: rural visibility

Subject:

Possible guest idea: rural businesses and visibility from their own ground

Hi [Name],

I thought of your podcast because you often speak about small business in a way that does not flatten the human side of the work.

I would like to suggest a topic:

Why rural small businesses need their own kind of visibility.

The angle would be that many rural businesses are not behind. They often work from different ground: local trust, seasonal rhythms, small teams, place-based stories, and returning customers.

This could be useful for listeners who feel tired by marketing advice built for another kind of business life.

Possible talking points:

- why a website is not the destination, but a tool
- why more content is not always the answer
- how rural businesses can build visibility from their own ground
- where AI helps, and where clarity still comes first

Short bio:

Kirsi works with rural small businesses, cultural projects, and community-rooted brands. Her work through Luopua focuses on finding the red thread, removing noise, and making businesses easier to understand without making them louder.

If this feels relevant, I can send a shorter episode outline.

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 6. Speaking pitch example: AI helpers

Subject:

Talk idea: building AI helpers without losing the human part

Hi [Name],

I am writing with a talk idea for your event.

The topic is:

Building AI helpers without losing the human part

The talk would help small business owners understand how to use AI in a way that supports their real work instead of creating more noise.

Core idea:

AI does not replace clarity. It repeats what you give it.

The talk could cover:

- why prompts are not enough
- how to give an AI helper a clear role
- what source files the helper needs
- how to protect brand voice
- what should stay human

I think this fits audiences who are curious about AI, but do not want another tool that makes their work feel heavier.

If this feels relevant, I can send a short session description.

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 8. Project partner example: village route

Subject:

Possible collaboration around a Central Finland village route

Hi [Name],

I am working on an idea for a village-based route in Central Finland and thought of you because your work connects with local tourism and place-based experiences.

The project would bring together villages, small businesses, cultural sites, and local stories into a route that visitors could follow without flattening the character of each place.

I think there may be a natural connection with your work because the route needs partners who understand both visitor needs and local reality.

The possible role could be joining the first planning conversation and helping define what would make this useful for businesses.

Would you be open to a short call about this?

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 13. Pitch subject line library

Use:

- Possible guest idea: [topic]
- Talk idea for [event]: [topic]
- Possible collaboration around [project]
- Sponsor idea for [event]
- A possible connection between [project] and [recipient]
- Invitation to join [project]
- Question about [specific role]
- Follow-up on [topic]

Avoid:

- Quick question
- Partnership opportunity
- Collaboration request
- Exciting chance
- Let’s join forces
- Unique opportunity
- Win-win partnership

## 15. Pitch quality check

Before sending a pitch, check:

- Is the topic clear?
- Is the recipient fit real?
- Is the message short enough?
- Is there one ask?
- Is the audience value clear?
- Is the bio relevant?
- Is there any fake praise?
- Does the next step feel easy?

## 16. Final rule

A pitch should make the fit visible.

Not force it.
