# Luopua Brand DNA

## 1. What Luopua by Kirsi is

Luopua by Kirsi is the master brand for Kirsi’s services, products, future tools, and public work.

Luopua means to let go.

The brand stands for letting go of excess and finding calm clarity in work and nature.

Every part of the brand should feel:

- organic
- unhurried
- clear
- grounded
- natural
- useful

## 2. Core idea

Let go of what hides the point.

## 3. Brand promise

Luopua by Kirsi helps people make their work easier to understand by removing what is unnecessary and finding the red thread.

## 4. Brand architecture

The architecture is:

```text
One house, many rooms.
```

There is one master brand:

```text
Luopua by Kirsi
```

Services use the master brand directly.

Products live inside the same house and receive their own short Finnish nature-word names.

Everything should still feel recognisably “by Kirsi.”

## 5. Master brand

Use the master brand for:

- web design
- content marketing
- coaching
- clarity sessions
- brand clarity
- website clarity
- AI helper setup
- client work
- invoices
- documents
- general public communication

Services do not need separate product names.

Use:

```text
Luopua by Kirsi
```

## 6. Product naming

Products use:

- short Finnish nature words
- 1–3 syllables
- lowercase in wordmarks
- easy enough for non-Finnish speakers to pronounce
- meaning that hints at the product’s purpose

Current product:

```text
polku
```

Polku means path.

It fits a nature planner and journal because it suggests planning your way forward.

Possible future product names:

- havu
- sammal
- lähde
- oksa
- pesä
- virta

Do not name every idea.

A product name should be used only when the thing is clearly a product, not a service, experiment, or internal workflow.

## 7. Lockup logic

Product lockup pattern:

```text
[master mark]

polku
Luopua by Kirsi
```

Rules:

- The byline is always “Luopua by Kirsi.”
- Do not abbreviate the byline.
- The product wordmark is larger.
- The byline is smaller and quieter.
- On very small surfaces, the master mark alone is enough.

## 8. The mark

The hand-drawn mark is the Luopua by Kirsi master brand mark.

It belongs to the master brand, not to one product.

Use it across products, documents, invoices, and public materials.

Do not:

- stretch it
- rotate it
- redraw it
- add effects
- make it into a decorative symbol disconnected from Kirsi’s hand

The mark should feel like Kirsi’s hand.

Not like borrowed symbolism.

## 9. Shared visual foundation

This foundation stays consistent across all products and services.

| Element | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Logo | hand-drawn master mark |
| Background | misty off-white `#F4F6F1` |
| Text | deep forest `#2E3B2C` |
| Muted text | `#6B7A66` |
| Greens | sage `#8DA383`, light sage `#D8E0D0` |
| Headings and wordmarks | Vollkorn 500–600 |
| Body | Inter 400/700 |
| Shape | soft and rounded |
| Illustrations | hand-drawn line SVGs, single colour, round caps |

Avoid beige or warm cream as the base background.

The background should lean green.

## 10. Product accents

Each product may own one accent colour.

The accent should come from Finnish nature.

Use accent colours only for actions such as buttons and links.

Current product accent:

```text
Polku = puolukka #9C4A41
```

Future examples:

- järvi teal
- kaarna brown
- hilla amber
- kanerva heather

Do not use accent colours as large decorative surfaces.

## 11. Illustration direction

Illustrations and icons should feel:

- hand-drawn
- organic
- slightly irregular
- calm
- simple
- single-colour
- made with lines, not filled blocks

Avoid:

- emojis
- perfect geometry
- decorative icon packs
- over-polished vector style
- symbols that feel pasted from another culture

## 12. Philosophy boundary

Japanese philosophies can stay as inner working principles.

They should not lead the public brand.

Do not say:

```text
Luopua is inspired by Japanese philosophy.
```

Say:

```text
Luopua means letting go.
In practice, that means removing what hides the point.
```

## 13. Philosophy as practice

Use philosophy as behaviour, not decoration.

### Wabi-sabi

Internal use:

Keep useful imperfection, age, texture, and human marks.

Public expression:

```text
Not polished smooth.
Present.
```

### Ma

Internal use:

Leave space.

Public expression:

```text
Leave room for the thought.
```

### Kanso

Internal use:

Remove excess.

Public expression:

```text
Remove what hides the point.
```

### Ikigai

Internal use:

Test fit between person, work, need, and livelihood.

Public expression:

```text
The right offer should fit the business, the person running it, and the people it serves.
```

## 14. What Luopua protects

Luopua protects:

- clarity
- calm
- nature connection
- human voice
- useful imperfection
- grounded visibility
- the right size of work
- service boundaries
- the red thread
- Kirsi’s own thinking

## 15. What Luopua rejects

Luopua rejects:

- excess
- hype
- fake urgency
- decorative philosophy
- borrowed depth
- making small work look bigger than it is
- making rural businesses feel behind
- AI that flattens voice
- visual systems that feel pasted on top
- community as decoration

## 16. Brand tension

The tension Luopua works with:

People are often told to add more.

More content.
More tools.
More platforms.
More strategy.
More polish.

Luopua asks:

What can be let go?

What still carries the work?

## 17. Emotional direction

Luopua should make the right person feel:

- calmer
- seen
- less pressured
- clear enough to continue
- safe to bring the messy version
- close to nature and real work

Not dazzled.

Not pushed.

Not managed.

## 18. Final rule

Luopua by Kirsi is not a borrowed aesthetic.

It is Kirsi’s way of seeing:

let go,
find the point,
leave enough space for what matters.
