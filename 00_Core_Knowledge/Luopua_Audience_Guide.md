# Luopua Audience Guide

## 1. Purpose

This guide defines who Luopua by Kirsi speaks to and how to respect them.

The audience is not only a market.

It is people carrying work, place, care, ideas, and sometimes too much noise.

## 2. Primary service audience

Luopua services mainly speak to:

- rural small business owners
- women-led microbusinesses
- solo entrepreneurs
- cultural operators
- village associations
- community projects
- place-based hospitality operators
- small organisations with limited time and scattered communication
- people curious about AI but wary of generic output

## 3. Product audience

Luopua products can speak to a wider group when the product has a clear purpose.

For example:

```text
polku
= nature planner and journal
= for people who want nature, planning, and reflection to travel with them
```

Products should still feel connected to the master brand:

- nature
- calm
- clarity
- organic rhythm
- soft structure
- one clear path forward

## 4. What the service audience often carries

They may carry:

- the whole business alone
- too many roles
- unclear service language
- an outdated website
- social media pressure
- local expectations
- seasonal workload
- volunteer work
- community responsibility
- price sensitivity
- worry about using AI without losing voice

## 5. What the product audience may need

They may need:

- a calm planning structure
- nature connection
- a soft rhythm
- reflection without pressure
- space to notice
- something useful and beautiful
- a product that feels natural, not productivity-obsessed

## 6. What they may have tried

They may have tried:

- website builders
- content calendars
- social media templates
- online courses
- Canva templates
- AI prompts
- brand worksheets
- productivity planners
- journaling systems
- planning apps
- copying larger businesses
- doing everything alone

Some of it may have helped.

Much of it may not have fit.

## 7. What they need from Luopua

They need:

- clearer words
- fewer decisions
- practical next steps
- respectful help
- grounded visibility
- tools with a clear job
- offers that are easier to explain
- content that grows from real work
- products that feel calm and usable
- AI source material that protects voice
- websites that know what they carry

## 8. What they do not need

They do not need:

- shame
- hype
- pressure
- another giant system
- a brand that makes them look like someone else
- daily posting demands
- guru advice
- borrowed language
- vague empowerment talk
- tools before clarity
- spiritual or philosophical language used as decoration

## 9. Key belief shifts

### Shift 1

From:

I am bad at marketing.

To:

The advice may not fit my business reality.

### Shift 2

From:

I need to post more.

To:

I need one clearer idea that content can carry.

### Shift 3

From:

I need a new website.

To:

I need to clarify what the website should help people understand.

### Shift 4

From:

AI sounds generic, so it is useless.

To:

AI needs better source material and clearer role boundaries.

### Shift 5

From:

I need to become more productive.

To:

I may need a rhythm that lets me notice what matters.

## 10. Rural small business reality

Rural businesses may work through:

- local trust
- return customers
- seasonal rhythms
- word of mouth
- limited teams
- local networks
- strong place identity
- practical service
- relationships built over years
- customers who know the owner before the website

Do not treat this as backward.

This is context.

## 11. Cultural and community work reality

Cultural and community projects may involve:

- volunteers
- limited funding
- shared responsibility
- invisible work
- unclear ownership
- many stakeholders
- public value that does not show as simple profit
- local memory
- place-based meaning

Do not flatten this into project language.

Name the human work.

## 12. How to speak to the audience

Use:

- respect
- plain language
- concrete examples
- calm confidence
- practical next steps
- honest boundaries
- nature as lived context, not decoration

Avoid:

- “you should”
- “every business must”
- “just”
- “simply”
- “all you need to do”
- “if you are serious”
- “stop playing small”
- “show up consistently”
- “dream clients”
- “ancient wisdom”
- “holistic journey”

## 13. Reader recognition lines

Useful lines:

You are not bad at marketing.

You may be trying to build visibility on ground that was never yours.

You may not need another tool.

You may need a clearer base for the tools you already use.

Your business does not need to look bigger.

It needs to become easier to understand.

You do not need to fill every space.

Some space is part of the work.

## 15. Audience checklist

Before writing, check:

- Who is this for?
- What are they carrying?
- What have they already tried?
- What false belief might they have?
- What would help them feel seen without being flattered?
- What is the smallest useful next step?
- Does the language respect their reality?
- Is nature used as real ground, not decoration?

## 16. Final rule

The audience is not behind.

The work is to build communication that fits the ground they stand on.
