# Verso – Beta Testing Playbook

## 1. Purpose

This playbook defines how Verso designs beta tests for Luopua offers and product rooms.

Beta is not a soft launch with prettier words.

Beta is a learning container.

## 2. Beta principle

Beta before polish.

The beta should test the offer, the process, the result, the proof, and the next step.

## 3. Recommended beta: Luopua Selkeytys

### Name

Luopua Selkeytys Beta

### For

Rural small businesses, solo entrepreneurs, and village-based service providers whose message, website, or content feels unclear.

### Format

90-minute session plus one-page clarity note.

### Core promise

```text
You leave with the sentence your business has been trying to say.
```

### Outcome

The client leaves with:

- one core message
- one audience definition
- one offer angle
- one simple visibility plan
- three next content ideas
- one next step for website or profile

## 4. Beta size

Start with:

```text
3 to 5 clients
```

This is enough to see patterns.

Do not overfill the beta.

## 5. Beta goals

The beta should learn:

- who benefits most
- what pain they use in their own words
- whether the promise is clear
- whether 90 minutes is enough
- whether the intake questions work
- whether the delivery note is useful
- what proof can be collected
- what next offer they naturally need
- where the process feels too heavy

## 6. Beta offer frame

Use this frame:

```text
This is a beta version of Luopua Selkeytys.

It is for small businesses whose message, website, or content feels scattered.

You bring rough material.

We work for 90 minutes.

You receive a one-page clarity note.

In return for the beta price, I will ask for feedback about what helped, what was unclear, and what next step you would want after this.
```

## 7. Beta boundaries

The beta is not:

- full brand strategy
- full website rebuild
- full copywriting
- content calendar factory
- tech setup
- unlimited support
- free consulting after delivery

Use this boundary:

```text
The beta gives one clear working direction.
It does not solve the whole business.
```

## 8. Beta invite criteria

Choose beta clients who:

- fit the audience
- have a real business or project
- have messy material to bring
- are able to give feedback
- are not expecting a full agency project
- respect boundaries
- might become proof or future paid work

Avoid beta clients who:

- want everything for free
- need urgent rescue
- want hype or fast growth promises
- have no material at all
- expect unlimited help

## 9. Beta workflow

```text
Invite
↓
Short intake
↓
Material review
↓
90-minute session
↓
One-page clarity note
↓
Feedback request
↓
Proof extraction
↓
Offer refinement
```

## 10. Before beta starts

Prepare:

- one invitation message
- one simple offer description
- 5-question intake
- session flow
- one-page delivery template
- feedback questions
- proof permission question
- next-step options

Do not build a full website before the beta.

## 11. During beta

Track:

- exact words clients use
- repeated confusions
- what they misunderstood about the offer
- what made them feel seen
- what part of the session created the strongest shift
- what took too long
- what was unnecessary
- what they asked for next

## 12. After each beta client

Create a short beta note:

```text
Client type:
[Short description]

Came in saying:
[Client words]

Unclear before:
[What was unclear]

Shift:
[What changed]

Sentence created:
[Core message]

Next action:
[What became clear]

Proof:
[Quote or observation]

What to improve:
[Process note]

Next offer signal:
[What they may need next]
```

## 13. Feedback questions

Ask:

1. What felt most useful?
2. What felt unclear or unnecessary?
3. What sentence or idea stayed with you?
4. What can you do now that felt harder before?
5. What would you want help with next?
6. May I use a short anonymous quote or case note from this session?

## 14. Proof permission wording

Use:

```text
May I use a short anonymous quote or case note from this session?

I can remove identifying details, use only your first name, or keep it fully anonymous.
```

## 15. Post-beta review

After 3 to 5 clients, review:

- strongest buyer
- clearest problem
- strongest promise
- best intake question
- strongest session moment
- most useful deliverable
- repeated next step
- price confidence
- what to remove
- what to launch publicly

## 16. Final rule

The beta is successful if Luopua learns what is true.

Not if it looks finished.
