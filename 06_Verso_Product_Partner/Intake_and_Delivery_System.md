# Verso – Intake and Delivery System

## 1. Purpose

This file defines the lean intake and delivery system for Luopua front-door offers.

The system should help the client feel seen.

It should not make the client work harder for no reason.

## 2. Core rule

Do not ask for data you will not use.

Every intake question must pass this test:

```text
What will this answer change?
```

If the answer is unclear, remove the question.

## 3. Intake is trust-building

If a client fills in a form, their words must appear in the session.

Example opening:

```text
You wrote that your business feels clear in your head but unclear on the page. That is where we will start.
```

The client should feel:

```text
She read what I sent.
```

## 4. Minimum useful client stack

Use:

- one intake form
- one shared folder
- one working document
- one call recording or notes file
- one delivery page
- one clear next-step message

Do not add more unless it removes confusion.

## 5. Luopua Selkeytys intake

Use 5 questions.

### Question 1

What feels unclear in your business message right now?

Why it matters:

Names the current pain in the client’s own words.

### Question 2

What do people often misunderstand about what you do?

Why it matters:

Shows where the current message fails.

### Question 3

What kind of client or work do you want more of?

Why it matters:

Shows desired direction.

### Question 4

What kind of client or work do you want less of?

Why it matters:

Shows boundaries and misfit.

### Question 5

Send one sentence, page, post, or offer description that does not feel right.

Why it matters:

Gives raw material.

### Optional Question 6

What have you tried that did not make things clearer?

Why it matters:

Shows failed tools, advice, or assumptions.

## 6. Questions to avoid

Avoid generic questions unless there is a clear reason:

- What are your brand values?
- What makes you different?
- What is your mission?
- Who is your ideal client?
- What is your vision?
- What is your brand personality?

These often produce generic answers.

Use sharper questions that reveal real tension.

## 7. Pre-session preparation

Before the session, Verso should help Kirsi prepare:

```text
Client words:
[exact phrases]

Repeated tension:
[what appears more than once]

Likely surface problem:
[website/content/offer/etc.]

Likely deeper problem:
[message/audience/offer/fit/etc.]

Possible red thread:
[draft]

Questions to ask:
[3–5 questions]
```

## 8. 90-minute session flow

Suggested flow:

```text
1. Client language review – 10 min
2. Problem naming – 15 min
3. Audience and buyer situation – 15 min
4. Offer angle – 20 min
5. Core message draft – 20 min
6. Next actions – 10 min
```

Keep the session focused.

Do not turn it into a full brand workshop.

## 9. Session opening

Start by proving the intake was used.

Use:

```text
You wrote that [client phrase].
That feels like the core tension we should start with.
```

This builds trust faster than a long intro.

## 10. One-page clarity note

Delivery should include:

```text
What was unclear
What matters now
Core message
Buyer description
Offer angle
Three content ideas
One next website or profile fix
Suggested next step
```

## 11. Delivery format

Suggested structure:

```text
Luopua Selkeytys Note

1. What you brought
2. What was unclear
3. What matters now
4. The sentence
5. Buyer description
6. Offer angle
7. Three content ideas
8. One website/profile fix
9. Suggested next step
```

## 12. Delivery boundary

The delivery note is not:

- full brand strategy
- full copy deck
- visual identity
- website wireframe
- content calendar
- full AI system

It is a clarity note.

## 13. Follow-up message

Aava can turn the delivery into client care.

Recommended message:

```text
Here is your Luopua Selkeytys note.

Start with the sentence and the one next website/profile fix.

You do not need to apply everything at once.

The point is to make the next decision clearer.
```

## 14. Proof collection built into delivery

After delivery, collect:

- what felt useful
- what became clearer
- what sentence stayed with them
- what they can do now
- what they want help with next
- permission to use a quote or anonymous case note

Do not leave proof collection as an afterthought.

## 15. System improvement after each client

After each delivery, update:

- intake questions
- session flow
- delivery template
- offer description
- FAQ
- proof notes
- next-step offer

## 16. Tool test

Before adding a tool to this process, ask:

- Does it reduce confusion?
- Does it improve the client moment?
- Will Kirsi use what it creates?
- Does it reduce work later?
- Does the client understand it?

If no, remove it.

## 17. Final rule

The process should make the client feel seen because their words shaped the work.
