# Verso – Product Development Playbook

## 1. Purpose

This playbook teaches Verso how to shape Luopua offers and products.

Verso does not start with a full product ecosystem.

Verso starts with one useful test.

## 2. Main workflow

Use this workflow:

```text
Seed
↓
Need
↓
Front door
↓
Minimum useful version
↓
Beta or first test
↓
Proof
↓
Refinement
↓
Next room
```

## 3. Step 1: Seed

A seed can be:

- repeated client question
- messy internal method
- service idea
- content theme
- beta idea
- product name
- client pain point
- tool idea
- proof from a case
- delivery process that keeps repeating

A seed is not automatically a product.

## 4. Step 2: Need

Name the real need.

Ask:

- What confusion does this reduce?
- Who feels this problem?
- What have they tried?
- What is the cost of leaving it unclear?
- What would feel lighter after the offer?
- What words do clients already use for this?

Weak need:

People need branding.

Stronger need:

The business feels clear in their head but unclear on the page.

## 5. Step 3: Front door

Choose one front door.

A front-door offer is the easiest first paid way to experience Luopua's thinking.

It should be:

- clear
- small
- useful
- low-friction
- easy to explain
- connected to a next step
- strong enough to collect proof

Current recommended front door:

```text
Luopua Selkeytys
```

A focused clarity session for a small business whose message, website, or content feels scattered.

## 6. Step 4: Minimum useful version

Use the smallest version that can create a real result.

Ask:

- What can we deliver in one focused session?
- What does the client leave with?
- What must be excluded?
- What proof can this produce?
- What would make the next offer easier to sell?

Example:

Not:

Full brand strategy, website plan, content system, and AI setup.

But:

90-minute clarity session plus one-page clarity note.

## 7. Step 5: Beta or first test

Beta comes before polish.

A beta should test:

- the promise
- the intake
- the session flow
- the delivery note
- the buyer fit
- the strongest shift
- the proof potential
- the next paid step

Suggested beta size:

```text
3 to 5 clients
```

## 8. Step 6: Proof

Collect:

- client words
- repeated pain points
- before and after examples
- screenshots when allowed
- short case notes
- objections
- client questions
- proof that the process helps
- next services clients ask for

Do not wait until after the beta to think about proof.

Build proof collection into the process.

## 9. Step 7: Refinement

After each beta, ask:

- What did the client come in saying?
- What was actually unclear?
- What shifted?
- What sentence was created?
- What next action became clear?
- What surprised them?
- What would they pay for next?
- What should be removed from the process?

## 10. Step 8: Next room

Only after proof, decide the next room.

Possible next rooms:

- Tila
- Jälki
- Kutsu
- AI helper setup
- productised template
- Polku-adjacent product
- light ongoing support

Do not build the whole ladder before the first offer is tested.

## 11. Service, offer, product, or internal system?

Use this distinction.

### Service

Custom client work under Luopua by Kirsi.

### Offer

A packaged service with clear scope and outcome.

Example:

Luopua Selkeytys, 90 minutes plus one-page clarity note.

### Product room

Repeatable product with its own name inside Luopua by Kirsi.

Example:

polku.

### Internal system

A tool or process that helps Kirsi deliver better work.

Example:

Luopua AI Team.

Do not confuse internal systems with public products.

## 12. Tool rule

Every tool must earn its place.

Before adding a tool, ask:

- Does this help Kirsi do better work?
- Does this reduce confusion for the client?
- Does this reduce confusion for Kirsi?
- Does this remove a real bottleneck?
- Will the answer, file, or data be used?
- Will the client understand what happens next?

If no, do not add the tool.

## 13. Lean working environment

Minimum useful setup:

- one intake form
- one shared folder
- one working document
- one call recording or notes file
- one delivery page
- one clear next-step message

Avoid:

- too many databases
- too many tags
- too many statuses
- AI workflows that create more reading than decisions
- tools that need maintenance but do not create value

## 14. Product naming rule

Use a product name only when the thing is truly a product room.

Product names should be:

- short Finnish nature words
- 1–3 syllables
- easy enough to pronounce
- connected to the product purpose
- inside Luopua by Kirsi

Do not product-name a service too early.

## 15. Verso decision test

Before recommending a product direction, answer:

```text
Is this the front door?
Is this the next room?
Is this only a seed?
Is this internal infrastructure?
Is this too early?
```

## 16. Final rule

Do not grow the tree before the seed has taken root.
