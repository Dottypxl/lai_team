# Verso – Product Ladder Guide

## 1. Purpose

This file defines how Luopua offers and product rooms can grow without becoming a scattered service menu.

The ladder should grow from one clear front door.

## 2. Core ladder principle

Do not sell the full ladder first.

Sell the first step.

Then build the next step from what people ask for next.

## 3. Current recommended ladder

```text
1. Luopua Selkeytys
2. Tila
3. Jälki
4. Kutsu
5. Luopua OS support
```

This is a working ladder, not a fixed law.

## 4. Step 1: Luopua Selkeytys

### What it is

A 90-minute clarity session.

### Purpose

Front-door offer.

### For

Small businesses whose message, website, or content feels scattered.

### Client brings

- website
- Instagram
- brochure
- current text
- one unclear offer or page
- five intake answers

### We clarify

- what they really offer
- who it is for
- what the buyer needs to understand first
- what makes the offer feel true
- what to say next

### Client leaves with

- one core message
- one offer angle
- one buyer description
- three content ideas
- one next step for website or profile

### Core promise

```text
You leave with the sentence your business has been trying to say.
```

## 5. Step 2: Tila

### What it is

One-page website or landing page built on the clarified message.

### Purpose

Turn clarity into own-ground presence.

### Best after

Luopua Selkeytys or equivalent clarity base.

### Boundary

Do not sell Tila before the message is clear.

## 6. Step 3: Jälki

### What it is

Deeper website and content foundation.

### Purpose

Build a stronger own-ground base for a small business with more material.

### Best after

A clear offer, audience, and message direction.

### Boundary

Do not let Jälki become unlimited content production.

## 7. Step 4: Kutsu

### What it is

Larger brand, website, and visibility package.

### Purpose

For clients who need deeper structure, copy, and public communication.

### Best after

Proof that the client values clarity work and has enough material for a larger project.

### Boundary

Needs clear scope and payment structure.

## 8. Step 5: Luopua OS support

### What it is

Ongoing support for content, AI-assisted workflows, or campaign rhythm.

### Purpose

Help clients keep clarity alive after the first build.

### Boundary

Not constant access.

Not daily content production.

Not open-ended tech support.

## 9. Product rooms

Product rooms are different from services.

A product room should be:

- repeatable
- ownable
- named
- connected to a specific user need
- inside Luopua by Kirsi
- supported by proof or repeated use

Current product room:

```text
polku
```

Possible future product rooms:

- havu
- sammal
- lähde
- oksa
- pesä
- virta

Do not use product names before the thing is ready to act like a product.

## 10. Product room readiness check

A product room is ready when these are clear:

```text
Name:
Meaning:
User:
Use case:
Format:
Core promise:
Shared Luopua foundation:
Own accent or feature:
Boundary:
Proof or reason:
```

## 11. When an offer should stay a service

Keep it as a service when:

- it is highly custom
- it depends on Kirsi’s judgement
- delivery changes by client
- process is still being tested
- pricing is not stable
- proof is not collected yet
- it is not repeatable enough

Example:

AI Helper Setup may stay a service first.

It can become a product room later only after repeated delivery and clear proof.

## 12. When an offer can become productised

Productise when:

- the same problem repeats
- the same process works
- the delivery can be named
- the output is repeatable
- the buyer understands the value
- proof exists
- the boundary is clear
- the product can live inside Luopua by Kirsi

## 13. Final rule

The ladder should grow from the work.

Not from the fear that Luopua needs to offer everything.
