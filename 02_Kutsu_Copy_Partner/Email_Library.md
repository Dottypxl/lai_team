# Kutsu – Email Library

## 1. Purpose

This file gives Kutsu email patterns for Luopua.

Emails should feel like clear notes from the work.

Some emails build trust.

Some explain an offer.

Some invite a decision.

None should pressure the reader with fake urgency.

## 2. Email types

Kutsu helps write:

- nurture emails
- sales emails
- offer announcement emails
- follow-up emails
- inquiry replies
- booking confirmation copy
- abandoned checkout-style reminders
- subject lines
- short promo emails
- launch sequences

## 3. Luopua email rule

Every email needs one job.

Choose one:

- name a problem
- show a shift
- explain an offer
- answer a doubt
- invite a decision
- follow up gently
- confirm next steps

Do not make one email carry everything.

## 4. Default email structure

Use this structure:

```text
Subject idea

Opening

Real situation or observation

Shift

Offer or next step

Soft CTA
```

## 5. Nurture email pattern

Use when the goal is trust, not immediate sale.

### Template

Subject:

[Clear subject]

Email:

[Personal or grounded opening]

[Observation from work]

[The shift]

[Practical point]

[Soft ending]

### Example

Subject:

Before the website

Email:

I understand why people want the website first.

It feels concrete.

A page.
A link.
Something to show.

But often the website is not the missing piece.

The missing piece is the sentence that says what matters.

What do you offer?
Who is it for?
Why does it matter?
What should the reader do next?

Without those answers, the website has to guess.

And websites are bad at guessing.

That is why I start with clarity before pages.

## 13. Subject lines to avoid

Avoid:

- Last chance
- Don’t miss this
- Are you ready to transform?
- Your brand deserves better
- The secret to better content
- Elevate your visibility
- High-converting copy made easy
- Unlock your dream clients
- This changes everything

## 17. Email quality check

Before sending an email, check:

- Does it have one job?
- Is the subject clear?
- Does the opening feel human?
- Is the offer easy to understand?
- Is the CTA natural?
- Is there any fake urgency?
- Is the language concrete?
- Is the reader respected?
- Does this sound like Luopua?
- Can one paragraph be removed?

## 18. Final rule

A Luopua email should feel like a useful note.

Not a campaign shouting through the inbox.
