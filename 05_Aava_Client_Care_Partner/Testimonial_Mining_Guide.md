# Aava – Testimonial Mining Guide

## 1. Purpose

This file helps Aava extract useful testimonials and proof from feedback, transcripts, emails, forms, and client notes.

The goal is not to polish feedback into empty praise.

The goal is to find the phrases that show what changed.

## 2. What to look for

Look for client language that names:

- what felt unclear before
- what became clearer
- what changed in thinking
- what became easier
- what surprised them
- what they valued
- what they can now do
- what they no longer feel stuck with
- what words they used naturally

Strong testimonials often sound ordinary.

Do not remove all texture.

## 3. Testimonial types

### Clarity testimonial

Shows what became clearer.

Example source phrase:

Now I finally know what the website should carry.

### Relief testimonial

Shows reduced pressure.

Example source phrase:

I thought I needed more content, but now I see I needed one clearer idea.

### Voice testimonial

Shows that the client felt recognized.

Example source phrase:

This finally sounds like me, not like a marketing template.

### Process testimonial

Shows what the work felt like.

Example source phrase:

I could bring the messy version and still leave with something clear.

### Result testimonial

Shows a practical output.

Example source phrase:

I now have a clear offer summary I can use on my website and with AI.

## 4. Mining workflow

Use this workflow:

```text
Source material
↓
Strong phrases
↓
What changed
↓
Proof category
↓
Clean quote
↓
Permission needed
↓
Use case
```

## 5. Step 1: Source material

Aava may receive:

- client email
- survey answer
- call transcript
- chat message
- project review
- testimonial draft
- social comment
- voice note transcript
- feedback form

Do not invent missing praise.

Only use what is present.

## 6. Step 2: Strong phrases

Mark phrases that sound like the client.

Good phrases:

- “I know what the page should carry now.”
- “The offer no longer feels scattered.”
- “I stopped trying to sound bigger.”
- “The messy material started to make sense.”
- “This finally sounds like my business.”

Weak phrases:

- “Great service.”
- “Very professional.”
- “Highly recommend.”
- “Amazing experience.”

Weak phrases can be used, but they are less useful.

## 7. Step 3: What changed

Name the before and after.

Template:

```text
Before:
[What was unclear or hard]

After:
[What became clearer or easier]
```

Example:

Before:

The client thought the website was the main problem.

After:

The client understood the message needed clearer ground first.

## 8. Step 4: Proof category

Sort the proof into one of these:

- website clarity
- offer clarity
- voice clarity
- AI source material
- content direction
- rural visibility
- process experience
- emotional relief
- practical next step
- trust and recognition

## 9. Step 5: Clean quote

Clean lightly.

Do not rewrite into marketing language.

Allowed:

- remove filler
- fix grammar
- shorten
- keep meaning
- keep the client's own words

Not allowed:

- add claims
- make the result bigger
- change uncertainty into certainty
- turn feedback into a promise
- remove all human texture

## 10. Step 6: Permission

If using a client quote publicly, ask permission.

Use:

Would you be comfortable with me using this sentence as a testimonial?

I can use your first name only, business name, or keep it anonymous.

## 11. Step 7: Use case

A quote can be used in:

- service page
- offer page
- case study
- social post
- proposal
- FAQ
- website proof section
- email
- internal source file

Match the quote to the right context.

## 12. Testimonial extraction output

Use this format:

```text
Strong quote:
[quote]

Why it works:
[short reason]

Proof category:
[category]

Before / after:
[before and after]

Suggested use:
[where to use it]

Permission needed:
[yes/no/unknown]

Cleaned version:
[edited quote]
```

## 13. Example: clarity feedback

Source:

I thought I needed a new website, but after the session I realised the page was trying to carry too many unclear things. Now I know what the first sentence needs to do.

Output:

Strong quote:

Now I know what the first sentence needs to do.

Why it works:

It shows practical clarity and connects to website work.

Proof category:

Website clarity

Before / after:

Before: client thought the website itself was the problem.

After: client understood the message needed a clearer job.

Suggested use:

Website Clarity Review page or Clarity Session page.

Permission needed:

Yes.

Cleaned version:

I thought I needed a new website, but the session showed me what the page was trying to carry. Now I know what the first sentence needs to do.

## 14. Example: AI feedback

Source:

AI sounded like everyone else before. After we made the helper role and voice boundaries, it started giving me drafts I could actually use.

Output:

Strong quote:

AI sounded like everyone else before.

Why it works:

It names a common pain clearly.

Proof category:

AI source material and voice clarity

Suggested use:

AI Helper Setup page.

Cleaned version:

Before, AI sounded like everyone else. After we created the helper role and voice boundaries, I started getting drafts I could actually use.

## 15. Example: process feedback

Source:

I liked that I did not have to make everything tidy first. I could just send the messy version and you saw what mattered.

Output:

Strong quote:

I could just send the messy version and you saw what mattered.

Why it works:

It shows Luopua's process and reduces anxiety for future clients.

Proof category:

Process experience

Suggested use:

Clarity Session page or onboarding email.

Cleaned version:

I could send the messy version, and you saw what mattered.

## 16. What not to use

Do not use:

- private details
- sensitive client information
- criticism without consent
- identifiable project details without permission
- claims that imply guaranteed outcomes
- feedback that needs more context to be fair

## 17. Turning feedback into case study source

Use this format:

```text
Client situation:
[Context]

Starting problem:
[What was unclear]

Work done:
[What Luopua helped with]

Shift:
[What changed]

Client words:
[Quotes]

Possible case angle:
[How to frame it]
```

## 18. Testimonial quality check

Before using a quote, check:

- Is it specific?
- Does it show a change?
- Does it sound real?
- Is permission clear?
- Is the client identifiable?
- Is the claim safe?
- Does it fit the offer?
- Does it sound like Luopua?
- Has it been over-polished?

## 19. Final rule

The strongest testimonial is often not praise.

It is the sentence where the client names what became clear.
