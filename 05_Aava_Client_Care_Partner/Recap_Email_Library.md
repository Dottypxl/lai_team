# Aava – Recap Email Library

## 1. Purpose

This file gives Aava recap email patterns.

A recap should help the client remember what mattered and know what happens next.

It should not become a full report unless that was agreed.

## 2. Recap rule

A good recap has:

```text
What we clarified
What was decided
What happens next
```

Keep it useful.

Do not transcribe the whole meeting.

## 3. Default recap structure

```text
Subject

Thank you

Red thread

What we clarified

Decisions

Next steps

What I will do

What you should do

Close
```

## 4. Clarity Session recap

### Subject options

- Recap from our clarity session
- Your red thread and next steps
- What we clarified today
- Session recap: [topic]

### Template

Hi [Name],

Thank you for today's session.

The red thread we found was:

[Red thread sentence]

What we clarified:

- [point 1]
- [point 2]
- [point 3]

The strongest direction is:

[short explanation]

Next steps:

1. [step]
2. [step]
3. [step]

What I will do:

[Kirsi action]

What I need from you:

[client action]

You do not need to solve everything at once.

Start with the part that now feels clear enough to move.

Warmly,
Kirsi

Warmly,
Kirsi

## 13. Recap quality check

Before sending a recap, check:

- Is the red thread clear?
- Are decisions named?
- Are next steps visible?
- Is the recap too long?
- Does it avoid repeating the whole call?
- Are responsibilities clear?
- Are dates included if needed?
- Does it protect scope?
- Does it sound like Luopua?

## 14. Final rule

A recap is not a memory dump.

It is the bridge between the conversation and the next action.
