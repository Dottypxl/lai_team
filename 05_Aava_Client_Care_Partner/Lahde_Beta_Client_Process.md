  Asset 1 — Pre-Work Email

  When sent: After booking is confirmed. Before the session.
  Subject: Before our Lähde session

  ---
  We have a session booked. Before we meet, I want to give you a short exercise to work
  through.

  It takes about 20–30 minutes. There are no right answers. The rougher the better.

  The exercise below is not preparation in the usual sense. It is the first part of the
  work. When you send your answers back, I will read them before our session and use them
  as the starting point for our time together.

  Send your answers to me by [date — one day before the session].

  You can write in point form, full sentences, or a mix. You can also send a voice note if
  that is easier. Whatever comes out first is the most useful version.

  ---
  [Pre-work questions and reassurance note follow below — include both in the same email.]

  ---
  What happens after you send:

  I will read everything before we meet. At the start of our session, I will tell you what
  I noticed. We will spend 90 minutes finding, naming, and organising the material that is
  already in your work — and you will leave with a source document you can return to every
  time you write.

  If you have any questions before the session, send them my way.

  Asset 2 — Pre-Work Questions

  Six questions. Each answer shapes a specific section of the Lähde document.

  ---
  1.
  Tell me about one moment from your work this year that felt right — where you did what
  you do well.

  It can be small. A conversation, a day, a delivery, a customer reaction. Describe what
  happened.

  [This becomes: Stories]

  ---
  2.
  What do customers say when they thank you, explain your work to someone else, or describe
   what they got?

  If you remember exact words, write those. A paraphrase is fine too.

  [This becomes: Client language]

  ---
  3.
  Describe where and how your work actually happens.

  The place. The season. The rhythm of a typical week or day. What shapes how you work.

  [This becomes: Place and rhythm]

  ---
  4.
  What do you know about your field or your work that most people on the outside would not?

  Not a value or a mission statement. Something you have learned by doing this. Something
  that took time to see.

  [This becomes: What this business believes]

  ---
  5.
  What do you find yourself explaining again and again — to customers, to people who ask
  what you do, to people who almost buy but hesitate?

  [This becomes: Recurring questions]

  ---
  6.
  Send one thing you have written about your work that does not quite sound like you.

  A sentence from your website, a caption, a bio, a service description. Something that
  exists but feels off.

  [This feeds: thread anchor + shows where the gap is between the work and the words]

  ---
  Note on question 6: If nothing comes to mind immediately, skip it. Send whatever you
  have. It is not required.

  ---
  Asset 3 — Reassurance Note

  Included in the pre-work email, directly after the questions.

  ---
  A short note before you start:

  You do not need to prepare a polished version. The rough draft is the useful one. If your
   answers are scattered, partial, or feel like they miss something — send them anyway.
  Scattered material shows where the work actually lives. That is exactly what the session
  is for.

  If you get stuck on a question, skip it and move to the next. If you want to answer in
  list form, that works. If a voice note is easier than writing, send that.

  There is no wrong way to send this back.

  ---
  Asset 4 — Delivery Email

  When sent: With the Lähde source document, within 72 hours of the session.
  Subject: Your Lähde document

  ---
  Here is your Lähde source document.

  It holds what we found in our session — the stories, the client language, the beliefs,
  the place, the recurring questions, and the thread that runs through the work.

  Start here: Read the client language section first. The next time you write anything
  about your work — a caption, a website line, an offer description — read that section
  before you begin. Use the words that are already there.

  The document is yours to return to. It is not a plan. It is a source. The more you draw
  from it, the more familiar it becomes.

  ---
  One note on scope:

  The document holds the material we found. It is not a content calendar, a strategy plan,
  or a writing service. If you find yourself wanting the next step — help with a specific
  page, an offer description, or an AI helper setup — that is a separate conversation. Send
   me a message and we can look at what makes sense.

  ---
  Three questions, when you have a moment:

  1. Does the document feel like yours — does it sound like your actual work?
  2. What will you use first?
  3. Is there anything missing that should be here?

  You can reply to this email whenever it suits. No deadline.

  In a few weeks I will check in with one more question about how the document has been
  useful in practice.

  Asset 5 — Feedback Questions

  Three questions sent with the delivery email (see Asset 4).
  One follow-up question sent 2–3 weeks later.

  ---
  With delivery (immediate):

  1. Does the document feel like yours — does it sound like your actual work?
  2. What will you use first?
  3. Is there anything missing that should be here?

  ---
  Follow-up question (2–3 weeks later):

  Subject: A quick question about your Lähde document

  One question:

  Has the document been useful, and what did you use it for?

  A few words is enough.

  ---
  Why these questions and not others:

  Question 1 tests whether the document reflects real material or Kirsi's interpretation of
   it. If it does not feel like theirs, the excavation missed something.

  Question 2 tells Verso which section of the document is the actual front door — useful
  for adjusting session emphasis.

  Question 3 flags gaps in the document structure before the beta is complete.

  The follow-up question tests whether the document gets used at all, and what it gets used
   for. If no one uses it: the document format needs to change, not the promise.

  ---
  Asset 6 — Proof Permission Wording

  Two versions. One for the end of the session (spoken). One for the delivery email
  (written).

  ---
  Session version (spoken, at the close of the 90-minute session):

  One question before we finish. Would you be open to me using a short anonymous quote or
  case note from our session? I can remove any identifying details, use only your first
  name, or keep it fully anonymous — whatever you are comfortable with. You can also say
  no.

  ---
  Email version (included at the end of the delivery email, after the feedback questions):

  ---
  A small request:

  If you are happy with how the session went, I would like to ask whether I can use a short
   anonymous note or quote from our work together.

  I can remove identifying details, use only your first name, or keep it fully anonymous.
  If you are not comfortable with this, that is completely fine — the session is yours
  regardless.

  Just let me know in your reply.

  ---
  Boundary note for Kirsi:

  Do not use any material, quote, or case detail without explicit yes. If the client says
  yes to anonymous but not to named, honour that exactly. If they do not reply to this
  question, the answer is no.
